Who We Are

The U.S. Naval Sea Cadet Corps is a leadership development nonprofit for young people, ages 10 through 17. Sponsored by the Navy League of the United States and supported by both the U.S. Navy and Coast Guard, our passionate all-volunteer force leads our units. Every year our cadets participate in training opportunities such as scuba, field and medical training, photojournalism, military police science and sailing. When cadets join the U.S. Naval Sea Cadet Corps, they embark upon a path of lifelong leadership. The program will teach them how to lead groups of their peers successfully and will also inspire them to become leaders in their schools and communities. For more information, please visit Seacadets.org

Fequently Asked Questions

The Division has Monthly Drills that take place from September through June (approximately matches the school year). The Division will meet a full Saturday and Sunday once a month.

No, they arrive and depart daily. The Drills are approximately 8 hours each day (Saturday and Sunday)

Each Cadet will go to Recruit Training; usually during the summer. The training is challenging and age appropriate. No “Full Metal Jacket” here. The Cadets enjoy Recruit Training and have many positive memories from the experience.

No, there is no obligation to join the military at all. If they do choose to join then they can receive early promotions or potential college scholarships because they participated in the program.

We do have a physical fitness test that the Cadets perform multiple times during the year. We encourage the Cadets to have a healthy lifestyle that includes exercise. That being said, they do not need to be a “star athlete” to pass our physical fitness tests.

There is a one-time uniform deposit collected during initial enrollment. This deposit will be returned after the Cadet concludes the program and returns their uniforms. Our Supply Department will swap their uniforms as they grow. The Division does not provide footwear or undergarments.

The Cadets wear the uniform of the United States Navy with appropriate Sea Cadet identification. They are required to maintain the same grooming requirements as members of the Navy.

The Cadets are required to attend 75% of all Monthly Drill Days. This translates to missing only 5 days per yearly term (10 Month Drill)
